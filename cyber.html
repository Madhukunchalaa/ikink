What are the areas covered by Cyber Insurance?

Cyber insurance policies can be categorized into three primary segments: First Party Coverage, Third Party (Liability) Coverage, and Crime Coverage.

  • First Party Cover:

    • This offers protection against monetary losses incurred by your company due to a cyber event. This can include losses brought on by computer system outages, which have lost revenue, interruptions to operations, expenses incurred in preparing claims, and harm to your company’s image. It also pays for data recovery expenses and limited hardware replacement charges for your company.

  • Third-Party Cover:

    • This provides liability coverage to outside parties if your company has a cyber incident. Examples include the propagation of viruses and malware, data and privacy breaches, network security breaches, management culpability, and media liability.

  • Crime Cover:

    • When fraud and identity theft occur, this cover can be helpful. Invoice manipulation, cyber extortion, identity theft, telephone fraud, funds transfer fraud, and hacking are just a few of the crimes it can conceal. You might also get paid back if your clients fall victim to “push payment fraud” when they pay money to someone pretending to be you
