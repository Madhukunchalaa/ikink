Hotel Insurance

Hotel insurance majorly covers both liability and property coverage to help hotel businesses to safeguard themselves from lawsuits and claims that accuse them of harm/loss to others, and also cover their property for damages. A single policy may cover different coverages from buildings, other structures, equipment, and inventory to that covered from uncertain incidents to staff and guests as well.

Why Hotel Insurance Is Needed?

Any property carrying a business activity is advised to have their property insured. A hotel business carries both liability risks as well as the risk of fire accidents in general. No two businesses are the same, however, the use of multiple electronic and electrical appliances as a source for ignition, and on-premises food preparations are some of the common activities that leave your hotel business at high risk for electrical mishaps and fire accidents. From vandalism to accidents occurring due to intoxicated customers are other risks associated with hotel business, apart from standard slips, falls, and property damage that you should be cautious about.

Hotel business is a service sector with hospitality at its core, hence you are dealing with a number of clients and your staff on an everyday basis. In such a scenario having your hotel business covered for property, equipment, as well as public liability remains crucial for your business. Get hotel property insurance and hotel liability insurance tailored to your specific needs.

Liability Coverage

1. Public and product liability insurance

It provides a cover for all the obligations including property damage, and personal loss/injury that is caused due to the negligent action of you or your employees to your clients or any third-party, due to your business activities. This also includes the food you have supplied or served resulting in a mishap. Public and product liability insurance comes as part of a commercial lease document for hotel operators.

A holistic cover against claims done by the guests for illness/food poisoning, pollution coverage, bodily injury, or property damage to clients that occurred in your hotel premises, is what liability insurance is all about.

2. Business interruption insurance

It is a policy that helps you cover an insured event and provide for the ongoing costs and safeguard the gross profit margins for the period of your business activity that got interrupted due to a natural disaster. This helps claim for business downtime and loss of income due to reasons like outages, equipment breakdown, and other unforeseen perils.

3. Employee practices liability

This helps the business to cover settlements, managementâ€™s defense costs, employee claims for breach, violation of laws, and any kind of claims from third parties that include past employees, vendors, contractors, clients, and others.

4. Cyber liability

Cyber liability insurance provides recovery from network privacy breaches and any kind of compromise on the data and information of the guests.

5. Employee dishonesty

This insurance covers you for any fraudulent activity by your employed staff with an intent to make financial gain through an act of dishonesty.

6. Liquor liability

If your hotel space has bars and offers liquor, liquor liability insurance gives cover for damage or injuries and claims demanded by customers.

7. Workers' compensation coverage

This helps to provide cover for any kind of medical expenses for illness/injury that occurred to your staff/employees while they are on the job or because of your hotel business. While workerâ€™s compensation insurance will provide a cover only for business hours, it is important for employees to look into injury insurance and income protection and how it can benefit them and their families.

8. Personal accidents and illness cover

If you are someone running a business under a proprietary holding (self-employed) or are part of the management, and if your State does not provide you a cover under the workerâ€™s compensation insurance, you are recommended to look out for personal accidents and illness cover and a business continuance expenses cover.

Accidents and illness cover can give you a financial cover of around 85% of your weekly income (if all terms are satisfied, and are applicable) for a said short-term period until you recover, and also claim for lump sum benefits if permanent damage occurs. Every event is unique, so it is highly recommended that you go through the PDS document carefully, with the help of an insurance broker who can simplify information for you.

9. Income protection insurance

Income protection insurance provides long-term cover for the time that you are unable to work and gives you accidental finance cover for a loss of income during your recovery period.

Property Coverage

1. Equipment & machine breakdown

Property insurance that helps to cover your expenses for the repair or replacement of equipment and machinery and also provides cover for the deterioration of stock following such a breakdown. Although machinery breakdown is the most common insurance undertaken by hotel operators, it also is complex in ways where some equipment may not be eligible for a cover, while others do. Therefore, it is highly important to read and know the level of coverage specified under the said policy you are wanting to take.

2. Food spoil/Deterioration of stock

Compensation for the loss/ food spoilage after an incident at the premises or as a result of machinery breakdown. Hotels have a lot of food stocked in cold storage and often face a huge financial burden not just with handling an accident/property loss, but also the impact of it due to loads of food wastage.

3. Auto liability insurance

For hotels that own vehicles to carry inventory and stock, which requires State-level mandates.
